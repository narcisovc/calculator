📌 Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field that enables machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. Within AI, we have Machine Learning (ML), Neural Networks (NN), and Deep Learning (DL).

📊 Machine Learning (ML)

ML is a subset of AI where machines learn from data to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed.

🔹 Common ML Algorithms:

  • Decision Trees
  • Support Vector Machines (SVM)
  • k-Nearest Neighbors (KNN)
  • Naive Bayes
  • Random Forest

🧠 Neural Networks (NN)

Neural Networks are a type of Machine Learning model inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. They consist of layers of interconnected nodes (neurons).

🔹 Common Neural Network Models:

  • Perceptron
  • Feedforward Neural Networks (FNN)
  • Radial Basis Function Networks (RBFN)

🔥 Deep Learning (DL)

Deep Learning is a subset of Neural Networks that involves multiple layers (deep networks) to process complex data representations.

🔹 Common Deep Learning Models:

  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN)
  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN)
  • Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM)
  • Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN)
  • Transformer Networks

🎯 Summary:
AI is the broadest field. Machine Learning is a subset of AI. Neural Networks are a specific type of ML, and Deep Learning is an advanced form of NN with multiple layers.
